‚ùå ‚úÖ Good vs Poor Prompts

‚ùå POOR PROMPT
"Write an email."

Why is it weak?

  • No audience context
  • No clear objective
  • No tone or style guidance
  • No length limits
‚úÖ GREAT PROMPT
"Draft a professional follow-up email for a client who attended our product demo three days ago. The email must: 1) Thank them for their time, 2) Summarize the top 3 points discussed, 3) Propose a follow-up call next week, 4) Maintain a friendly yet professional tone, 5) Stay under 200 words."

Why does it work?

  • ‚úÖ Clear context (client, recent demo)
  • ‚úÖ Specific objective (follow-up)
  • ‚úÖ Detailed structure (5 items)
  • ‚úÖ Defined tone (friendly professional)
  • ‚úÖ Word count limit (200 words)
‚ùå POOR PROMPT
"Summarize this document."

Why is it weak?

  • No target length
  • No priority topics
  • No desired format
‚úÖ GREAT PROMPT
"Analyze this 45-page contract and produce a 1-page executive summary that includes: 1) Key financial terms (in a table), 2) Primary obligations for each party (bullet list), 3) Important dates (timeline), 4) Potential risks flagged in red. Prioritize clauses related to intellectual property and confidentiality."

Why does it work?

  • ‚úÖ Specific length (1 page)
  • ‚úÖ Clear structure (4 sections)
  • ‚úÖ Detailed format (table, bullets, timeline)
  • ‚úÖ Clear priorities (IP & confidentiality)

üöÄ Advanced Prompting Techniques

üé≠ Role Playing

Assign a specific role to Copilot

Example:
"Act as a senior financial analyst with 15 years of experience in tech startups. Review this balance sheet..."
Benefit: Specialist-level, context-aware responses

üîó Chain of Thought

Ask Copilot to show step-by-step reasoning

Example:
"Explain step by step how you reach the conclusion, showing your reasoning at each stage..."
Benefit: More precise, verifiable answers

üéØ Few-Shot Learning

Provide examples of the desired output

Example:
"Generate titles similar to these:
- '10 Ways to Transform Your Workflow'
- '5 Secrets Experts Never Share'
Now produce 5 more for my article about..."
Benefit: Consistency in tone and format

üîÑ Iterative Refinement

Improve the answer in multiple passes

Workflow:
1. "Give me an initial draft..."
2. "Make it more technical..."
3. "Add supporting statistics..."
4. "Cut it down to 500 words..."
Benefit: Fine-grained control over the final output

üìä Structured Output

Define the exact structure you need

Example:
"Respond in JSON with these fields:
{
  'title': '',
  'priority': 1-5,
  'actions': []
}"
Benefit: Easy integration with other systems

‚ö†Ô∏è Constraint-Based

Set explicit boundaries and limits

Example:
"Generate ideas that:
- Can be implemented in < 2 weeks
- Cost under $5,000
- Do not require additional headcount"
Benefit: Responses aligned with real-world constraints

üíé Pro Tip

Combine multiple techniques in a single prompt for exceptional results. Example: Role Playing + Chain of Thought + Structured Output.
